Nick Kochornswasdi Gallery is pleased to present Paintings By Diego, an exhibition by the artist Diego Barrientos, his first with the gallery. Barrientos works with medium to large-scale canvases and utilizes a variety of different materials that are directly applied to the canvas to create mysterious textures that betray their unstretched form.

Paintings by Diego comprises a series of self-portraits created from 2018 to 2019. As the exhibition title’s literalness suggests, it is an exhibition of Barrientos’ paintings. Questioning his relationship between the self and the work, Barrientos frequently uses and presents his work as an actual product that one would use for comfort, warmth, and protection. Implying the work is deeply personal while still being straightforwardly accessible. The paintings remain unstretched in an effort to remind the viewer the materiality of the canvas and its potential functionality other than the art object, with the hope that the viewer themselves will make their own art, because art is for everyone.

The various shades of grey in his work references his time in the military and the experience of depersonalization. The shapes and forms express the implications of alienation, longing, desire, despair, and pain, through a sliver of humor. Multi-purpose parachute cords were applied directly onto the canvas and its skin-like textures suggest the self is also an object. The exhibition proposes an intense working of complex human emotions while contemplating for a sense of inclusivity and belonging.

Diego Barrientos (b. 1987, Guatemala City, Guatemala) received his MFA from the California Institute of the Arts in 2019 and a BA from California State University, Northridge in 2016. Recent exhibitions include Alienation, L-Shape Gallery, CalArts, Valencia, CA (2019); And Then There is California, Santa Monica, CA (2019); Other Places Art Fair, San Pedro, CA (2019); and Incognito, Institute of Contemporary Art, Los Angeles, CA (2018). Forthcoming exhibition includes The Life Cycle of Intimacy, New Wight Gallery, Los Angeles, CA. He currently lives and works in Los Angeles.
